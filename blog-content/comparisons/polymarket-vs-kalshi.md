## Two Leading Platforms

Polymarket and Kalshi represent different approaches to prediction markets. Polymarket operates as a crypto-native platform, while Kalshi is a CFTC-regulated exchange.

Both let you trade on event outcomes, but they differ significantly in structure, markets, and user experience.

## Regulatory Status

The most fundamental difference is regulatory approach.

**Kalshi** is a designated contract market (DCM) regulated by the CFTC. This means:
- US traders can use it with full legal clarity
- Stricter compliance requirements
- More limited market selection
- Traditional KYC requirements

**Polymarket** operates on blockchain technology with less regulatory oversight:
- Broader market selection
- Faster market creation
- Geographic restrictions for US users
- Crypto wallet required

Your location and regulatory preferences may determine which platform is accessible and appropriate.

## Market Selection

Market variety differs significantly between platforms.

**Polymarket** offers:
- Politics (elections, policy, international)
- Sports (major leagues, events)
- Entertainment (awards, media)
- Crypto and finance
- Science and technology
- Cultural events and more

**Kalshi** offers:
- Economics (inflation, interest rates)
- Politics (US elections, policy)
- Weather and climate
- Financial markets
- Some entertainment

Polymarket has broader selection; Kalshi focuses on events with clear, verifiable outcomes that regulators approve.

## Liquidity Comparison

Trading depth matters for execution quality.

**Polymarket** generally has:
- Higher overall volume
- Better liquidity on popular markets
- More active trader community
- Tighter spreads on major events

**Kalshi** offers:
- Solid liquidity on core markets
- Professional market maker support
- Consistent spreads
- Growing but smaller user base

For major events, both have adequate liquidity. For niche markets, Polymarket typically offers more activity.

## Fee Structure

Costs differ between platforms.

**Polymarket:**
- Trading fees are minimal
- Gas fees for blockchain transactions
- No withdrawal fees typically
- Overall low-cost structure

**Kalshi:**
- Transaction fees on trades
- No gas fees (not blockchain-based)
- Standard withdrawal processes
- Generally higher fee structure

Polymarket tends to be cheaper for active traders, though gas fees can vary.

## User Experience

Interface and accessibility differences:

**Polymarket:**
- Modern, intuitive interface
- Requires crypto wallet
- Mobile-friendly
- Strong community features
- Third-party tool ecosystem (like Alpha Whale)

**Kalshi:**
- Professional trading interface
- Traditional account structure
- Clean, straightforward UX
- Built-in charting tools
- Less third-party ecosystem

Both platforms are well-designed. Polymarket feels more consumer-oriented; Kalshi feels more institutional.

## Account Setup

Getting started differs between platforms.

**Polymarket setup:**
1. Create or connect crypto wallet
2. Fund wallet with USDC
3. Deposit to Polymarket
4. Start trading

**Kalshi setup:**
1. Create account with identity verification
2. Complete KYC process
3. Fund via bank transfer or card
4. Start trading

Kalshi's process is more traditional; Polymarket's requires crypto familiarity but can be faster.

## Settlement and Resolution

How markets resolve and pay out:

**Polymarket:**
- Decentralized resolution mechanisms
- Generally fast settlement
- Some disputes possible
- On-chain verification

**Kalshi:**
- Centralized resolution
- Clear, regulated process
- Predictable dispute handling
- Traditional payment rails

Both platforms resolve markets reliably, but mechanisms differ.

## Geographic Access

Access restrictions vary:

**Polymarket:**
- Available in most countries
- US access restricted
- VPN usage varies in policy
- Fewer restrictions overall

**Kalshi:**
- Primarily US-focused
- Full legal access for US residents
- Some international availability
- Clear regulatory standing

Your location significantly affects which platform is appropriate.

**Kalshi:**
