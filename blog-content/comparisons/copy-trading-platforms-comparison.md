## The Copy Trading Landscape

Copy trading has emerged as a popular way to participate in prediction markets. Multiple platforms offer copy trading services, each with different features, traders, and approaches.

Understanding your options helps you choose the platform that best fits your needs.

## What to Look for in Copy Trading Platforms

Before comparing specific platforms, consider the key evaluation criteria:

**Trader quality:** How good are the traders available to follow?

**Transparency:** Can you see detailed track records and understand trader strategies?

**Execution:** How closely are trades replicated? What's the latency?

**Fees:** What do you pay for the copy trading service?

**Platform reliability:** Is the system stable and trustworthy?

**User experience:** Is the platform easy to use and understand?

## DIY Solutions

Some traders build their own copy trading through APIs and manual tracking.

**Strengths:**
- Complete control over implementation
- No platform fees
- Can customize exactly to needs

**Considerations:**
- Requires technical ability
- Time-intensive to build and maintain
- Reliability depends on your own systems
- Miss out on curated trader selection

**Best for:** Technical users with specific requirements that platforms don't meet.

## Trader Selection Differences

How platforms curate traders varies significantly.

**Alpha Whale approach:**

- Provides analytics on trader styles and risks

**Other platforms may:**

## Fee Structures

Platforms monetize differently:

**Subscription fees:** Fixed monthly or annual cost

**Performance fees:** Percentage of profits generated

**Spread markup:** Slightly worse prices on trades

**Free with premium tiers:** Basic access free, advanced features paid

Understand the total cost and how it affects your net returns.

## Integration Considerations

## Due Diligence Process

4. **Monitor results** against claimed performance
