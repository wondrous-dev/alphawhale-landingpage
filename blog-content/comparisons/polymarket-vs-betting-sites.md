## Two Different Models

Polymarket and traditional betting sites both let you wager on events, but they work fundamentally differently. Understanding these differences helps you choose the right platform and approach.

## How Betting Sites Work

Traditional betting sites (sportsbooks, bookmakers) use a house model:

**Key characteristics:**
- You bet against the house
- The house sets odds
- House takes a margin (vig/juice)
- Odds fixed at time of bet
- Can't sell your bet before resolution

**Business model:** The house profits from the margin built into odds. Your win is their loss; your loss is their win.

## How Polymarket Works

Polymarket is a prediction market with a trading model:

**Key characteristics:**
- You trade against other users
- Prices determined by supply and demand
- Platform takes minimal fees
- Prices change continuously
- Can exit positions anytime before resolution

**Business model:** Platform earns from fees/spread. Traders win and lose against each other.

## Key Differences Explained

### Odds/Price Setting

**Betting sites:** Odds set by professional oddsmakers. Designed to attract balanced action while guaranteeing house profit.

**Polymarket:** Prices emerge from trading activity. Reflect collective beliefs of all market participants.

### Liquidity and Trading

**Betting sites:** Bet is placed, then you wait. No trading. Limited or no cash-out options.

**Polymarket:** Full trading throughout event lifecycle. Buy, sell, adjust positions as information changes.

### Information Efficiency

**Betting sites:** Odds update periodically based on house decisions. May lag real-time information.

**Polymarket:** Prices update continuously as traders react to news. More efficient information aggregation.

### Fee Structure

**Betting sites:** House margin built into odds (typically 5-10%+). Hidden in the lines.

**Polymarket:** Explicit, low fees. Better value for informed traders.

Several factors make Polymarket superior for informed traders:

**Price discovery:** Prices reflect crowd wisdom, not house positions.

**Trading flexibility:** Exit losing positions, lock in profits, adjust exposure.

**Lower costs:** Minimal fees compared to betting site margins.

**Information value:** Prices serve as probability estimates useful beyond trading.

## Advantages of Traditional Betting

Betting sites have some advantages:

**Simplicity:** Place bet, wait for result. No trading complexity.

**Guaranteed odds:** The line you take is the line you get.

**Regulated:** Often more clearly regulated in many jurisdictions.

**Bonuses:** Promotional offers common (though usually with strings attached).
