## Why Consider Alternatives?

Polymarket is the largest prediction market platform, but it's not the only option. Different platforms offer different markets, features, and user experiences.

Understanding alternatives helps you diversify across platforms, access different market types, and find the best fit for your trading style.

## Criteria for Evaluation

We'll compare platforms across several dimensions:

- **Market variety:** Types and breadth of available markets
- **Liquidity:** How easily you can trade at fair prices
- **User experience:** Interface and ease of use
- **Fees:** Trading costs and fee structures
- **Regulatory status:** Legal accessibility and compliance
- **Unique features:** What distinguishes each platform

## Kalshi

Kalshi operates as a CFTC-regulated prediction market in the United States.

**Strengths:**
- Fully regulated in the US
- Clean, professional interface
- Strong market depth on popular events
- No crypto knowledge required

**Weaknesses:**
- More limited market selection than Polymarket
- US-focused (some restrictions)
- Higher fees than decentralized alternatives

**Best for:** US traders who want regulatory protection and don't need exotic markets.

## Metaculus

Metaculus is a forecasting platform with a different model than traditional prediction markets.

**Strengths:**
- Strong community of forecasters
- Good track record on accuracy
- Educational resources
- No money required to participate

**Weaknesses:**
- No real-money trading
- Points-based rather than financial returns
- Different user experience than trading platforms

**Best for:** Those interested in forecasting skill development rather than financial returns.

## PredictIt

PredictIt is a political prediction market with a long history.

**Strengths:**
- Established track record
- Focus on political markets
- Academic partnerships
- US-accessible

**Weaknesses:**
- $850 per-contract investment limits
- 10% fee on profits plus 5% withdrawal fee
- More limited market selection
- Regulatory uncertainty

**Best for:** Political junkies who want regulated US access to election markets.

## Augur

Augur is a decentralized prediction market protocol.

**Strengths:**
- Fully decentralized
- Censorship-resistant
- Open protocol anyone can build on
- No platform fees (only network fees)

**Weaknesses:**
- Complex user experience
- Lower liquidity than centralized alternatives
- Requires crypto knowledge
- Slower settlement

**Best for:** Crypto-native users who value decentralization over convenience.

## Regulatory Considerations

Legal status varies by platform and jurisdiction.

**Know your local laws.** Prediction market legality differs by country and sometimes by state/region.

**Regulated platforms** like Kalshi offer more legal certainty but may have limitations.

**Crypto-based platforms** like Polymarket operate in a grayer area in some jurisdictions.

Consult appropriate advisors if you're unsure about legality in your location.
