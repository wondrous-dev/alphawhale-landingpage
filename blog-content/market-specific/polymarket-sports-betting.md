## How Sports Markets Work on Polymarket

Polymarket sports markets differ from traditional sportsbooks:

**Trading vs. betting:** You can buy and sell positions, not just place bets.

**Market-determined odds:** Prices reflect trader consensus, not bookmaker lines.

**Continuous trading:** Adjust positions as games progress or news emerges.

**Different coverage:** Market selection differs from traditional sportsbooks.

Several advantages exist:

Honest limitations:

**Smaller selection:** Traditional sportsbooks offer far more markets.

**Less coverage depth:** Prop bets and in-game options limited.

## Sports Trading Strategies

Strategies that work on Polymarket sports markets:

**Arbitrage:** Compare Polymarket prices to sportsbook lines for discrepancies.

**News trading:** React to injury news, lineup changes, or other developments.

**Momentum trading:** Championship odds shift with team performance.

**Contrarian plays:** When sentiment overreacts, odds may misprice.

## Risk Management for Sports

Sports markets carry specific risks:

**Event timing:** Games happen at scheduled times; news before games can be critical.

**Key player impact:** Injuries to star players dramatically affect outcomes.

**Variance:** Short-term sports outcomes have high variance.

**Correlation:** Multiple sports positions may correlate (betting multiple favorites, for example).
