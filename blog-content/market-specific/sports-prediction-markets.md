## Trading Strategies

Strategies for sports prediction markets:

**Preseason positioning:** Identify undervalued teams before season begins.

**In-season adjustment:** React to performance that differs from expectations.

**Injury arbitrage:** Trade quickly when injury news breaks.

**Momentum plays:** Follow hot teams or sell cold streaks.

**Value identification:** Compare prediction market prices to statistical models.

## Championship Market Dynamics

Championship markets have specific patterns:

**Preseason:** High uncertainty, wide price ranges.

**Early season:** Overreaction to small sample sizes.

**Mid-season:** Prices stabilize as trends emerge.

**Playoff push:** Prices converge as field narrows.

**Playoffs:** Highest intensity, binary outcomes each round.

## Managing Sports Prediction Risk

Sports trading carries inherent risks:

**Single elimination events:** One game can end championship hopes.

**Injury impact:** Key player injuries dramatically shift odds.

**Variance:** Sports outcomes have significant randomness.

**Correlated positions:** Multiple championship bets may correlate.

## League-Specific Considerations

Different sports have different dynamics:

**Football (NFL):**
- Small sample sizes
- High variance outcomes
- Injury impact enormous
- Playoff format matters

**Basketball (NBA):**
- Long season for evaluation
- Stars dominate outcomes
- Less variance than football
- Playoff matchups crucial

**Baseball (MLB):**
- Very long season
- High game-to-game variance
- Playoff randomness notable
- Pitching matchups matter

## Seasonal Timing

When you trade matters:

**Offseason:** Position on expected changes (trades, signings).

**Preseason:** Identify mispriced expectations.

**Early season:** React to surprises, fade overreactions.

**Mid-season:** Most stable prices, clearest information.

**Late season:** Trade on playoff positioning.

**Playoffs:** High intensity, binary outcomes.
